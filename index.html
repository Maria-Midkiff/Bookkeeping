Bookkeeper and CPA - Do I Need Both?

In the long run, using a bookkeeper will save you time and money. The better organized your records are, the easier it is for your CPA to do their job. Your CPA will have accurate records to do their work more efficiently, saving you from extra billable hours by them. Bookkeepers keep track of the details on a day-to-day basis so that your CPA can work with your data at a higher level.

W. Long handles every transaction and can mainstream much of your administrative work. A large part of what we do can be considered your "back office" function: compiling data and maintaining records, handling payroll, creating invoices, financial reconciliation, and providing those records to your CPA in the form they need.

What Records are Needed to Get Started?

Purchases, sales, payroll, and other transactions that occur in your business will generate documents that capture the event. Supporting documents include sales slips, paid bills, invoices, receipts, deposit slips, and canceled checks. Not only should they be retained, they need to be organized in a way so that they can be retrieved when needed. Storing them by year and type of transaction is a good example of this.
